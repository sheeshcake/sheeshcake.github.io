Manila, Nov. 5, 1998- The peso closed at a 24-week high against the U.S. dollar yesterday after a government official said the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas would refrain from intervention. The local currency rose further 0.9 percent yesterday to end at P39.40 to the American currency, its highest level since closing at P39.30=$1 on May 19. The peso reached an intra-day high of P39.155 to the dollar. In the past month alone it had risen 11.7 percent against the dollar. "The strengthening of our currency is a welcome relief for the nation,'' President Joseph Estrada's spokesman Fernando Barican told reporters shortly before the currency market closed. "The peso rise is the direct and natural result of market forces.'' He said the Monetary Board ``will not interfere in the continued appreciation of the peso in the last few days.'' "The good performance of the peso in the foreign exchange market can be traced to the country's sound macro-economic fundamentals and fiscal policies,'' Barican added. President Estrada explained that the surging peso was an indication of the rising confidence in his four-month-old administration. "It is proof of business confidence, particularly foreign investors who are now convinced that our economy is sound," he said in an interview over radio station DZRH. Many financial analysts attribute the peso's strength to inflow of foreign currency into the Philippines from foreign investors and the latest IMF approval of dollar loans to the Philippine government.

It wasn't a dream

by FRANZ KAFKA

One morning, when Gregor Samsa woke from troubled dreams, he found himself transformed in his bed into a horrible vermin. He lay on his armour-like back, and if he lifted his head a little he could see his brown belly, slightly domed and divided by arches into stiff sections. The bedding was hardly able to cover it and seemed ready to slide off any moment.

His many legs, pitifully thin compared with the size of the rest of him, waved about helplessly as he looked. "What's happened to me?" he thought. It wasn't a dream. His room, a proper human room although a little too small, lay peacefully between its four familiar walls. A collection of textile samples lay spread out on the table - Samsa was a travelling salesman - and above it there hung a picture that he had recently cut out of an illustrated magazine and housed in a nice, gilded frame. It showed a lady fitted out with a fur hat and fur boa who sat upright, raising a heavy fur muff that covered the whole of her lower arm towards the viewer. Gregor then turned to look out the window at the dull weather.
